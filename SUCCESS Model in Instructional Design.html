Six Principles of the SUCCESS Model:
Each principle contributes to making instructional content engaging and memorable:

  • Simple: Strip down content to its core message, ensuring it‚Äôs easy to understand and remember. Avoid overwhelming learners with unnecessary details by focusing on the essentials.

  • Unexpected: Capture attention with surprising elements or thought-provoking questions that engage curiosity. Breaking learners‚Äô expectations makes content more intriguing and memorable.

  • Concrete: Use specific examples, sensory details, and clear language that helps learners visualize concepts. Concrete information is easier to remember than abstract ideas.

  • Credible: Establish trust through credible sources, statistics, or testimonials. Providing credible information boosts confidence in the material, making it more likely to be retained.

  • Emotional: Connect with learners on an emotional level to increase engagement. Content that resonates with learners‚Äô values or emotions is more impactful and memorable.

  • Stories: Use storytelling techniques to present content in a narrative form. Stories create a structured context that aids understanding and retention, as learners connect emotionally with the characters or scenarios.

How Instructional Designers Use the SUCCESS Model:
By incorporating the SUCCESS principles, instructional designers can make training content more engaging and memorable, leading to improved knowledge retention and application:
  • Applying "Simple": Design lessons with a clear focus, emphasizing the main ideas. Use visual aids and summary slides to highlight key points without overwhelming learners.

  • Using "Unexpected": Incorporate unexpected elements such as surprising facts, real-world scenarios, or quizzes that challenge assumptions, keeping learners curious and engaged.

  • Making Content "Concrete": Use examples, analogies, and visuals that make abstract concepts tangible. For instance, in a project management course, use a visual timeline to represent task sequencing.

  • Ensuring "Credibility": Back up claims with data, research, and real-life success stories. Use expert quotes or testimonials where applicable to add authenticity.

  • Creating "Emotional" Appeal: Relate content to learners' goals, motivations, or real-life concerns. Show the personal or organizational impact of applying the skills being taught.

  • Incorporating "Stories": Develop case studies or scenarios that contextualize learning in a narrative format. This approach is especially effective in fields like customer service or healthcare, where interpersonal skills are essential.

Example Course Using the SUCCESS Model:
In a **Cybersecurity Awareness Training Program**, you can apply the SUCCESS principles as follows:
  • Simple: Break down complex cybersecurity practices into key actions that are easy to follow, like ‚ÄúThink before you click‚Äù or ‚ÄúVerify before you trust.‚Äù

  • Unexpected: Start the course with surprising statistics on cyber-attacks or a simulated phishing email that grabs attention and demonstrates the risks of not being vigilant.

  • Concrete: Use examples of real-life cybersecurity breaches and walk through specific steps of how they occurred, making abstract threats more relatable.

  • Credible: Include data from recent cybersecurity studies and quotes from experts on the importance of safe online practices to reinforce credibility.

  • Emotional: Emphasize the potential personal impact of cybersecurity risks, such as identity theft or financial loss, to connect emotionally with learners.

  • Stories: Share a story of an individual or company impacted by a cyber-attack, illustrating the consequences and the preventative steps that could have been taken.
