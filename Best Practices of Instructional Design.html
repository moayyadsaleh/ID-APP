Best Practices of Instructional Design

1. Consistency-driven: Maintaining uniformity in course design and delivery.
2. Technology-integrated: Utilizing various tech tools to enhance learning experiences.
3. Structured: Organizing content logically for better comprehension.
4. User-friendly: Ensuring intuitive navigation and accessibility in course materials.
5. Feedback-rich: Providing timely and constructive responses to learners.
6. Engagement-focused: Prioritizing interactive elements to captivate learners.
7. Assessment should be objectives-aligned: Ensuring assessments accurately reflect learning objectives.
8. Scaffolded: Gradually increasing complexity to support learner progression.
9. Chunked: Breaking down information into smaller, manageable sections.
10. Inclusivity-centered: Designing for diverse backgrounds and learning preferences.
11. Real-world-focused: Incorporating practical applications and scenarios.
12. Collaboration-enhancing: Fostering opportunities for peer interaction and teamwork.
13. Data-informed: Using analytics to guide instructional decisions and improvements.
14. Continuously improved: Regularly updating content based on feedback and effectiveness.
15. Easily digestible: Content that is simple to understand and absorb.
16. Interactive, Impactful, Effective, Results-Driven: Prioritizing active learning methods that engage learners in meaningful tasks, ensuring the instruction has a significant, lasting effect on knowledge retention, skill acquisition, and measurable outcomes.
17. Grounded in Adult Learning Theories: Incorporating principles from adult learning theories (such as andragogy), emphasizing experience-based, problem-solving, and goal-oriented learning approaches tailored to adult learners.
18. Learner-Focused: Addressing the unique needs, preferences, and motivations of adult learners, making the learning process relevant, practical, and aligned with their personal and professional goals.
19. Mobile-friendly and accessible: Ensuring course content is optimized for mobile devices and compliant with accessibility standards, allowing learners to engage with materials anytime, anywhere, and accommodating diverse needs, including those with disabilities.
