The 4Cs/4Ps of Instructional Design

The 4Cs/4Ps of Instructional Design:
â€¢ The 4Cs (Connection, Concept, Concrete Practice, and Conclusion) and 4Ps (Preparation, Presentation, Practice, and Performance) are complementary instructional design frameworks used to create effective and engaging learning experiences.

Implementing the 4Cs Model:
The 4Cs approach ensures a structured, learner-centered journey:

  • Connection:
    Begin by linking the content to learners' prior knowledge or experiences to create relevance and engagement.

  • Concept:
    Introduce the core concept or theory, focusing on essential information that learners need to grasp.

  • Concrete Practice:
    Provide hands-on activities and scenarios where learners can apply what they've learned in a practical context.

  • Conclusion:
    Summarize key takeaways and encourage reflection to reinforce understanding.

Implementing the 4Ps Model:
The 4Ps support learners' progressive development through structured stages:
  • Preparation:
    Set the stage for learning by preparing learners with foundational knowledge or pre-assessment activities.

  • Presentation:
    Deliver new content or skills in an organized, clear format to facilitate comprehension.

  • Practice:
    Encourage learners to practice through exercises or role-plays, building confidence and reinforcing skills.

  • Performance:
    Allow learners to demonstrate their mastery of the content in an assessment or real-world scenario.
