Understanding the Components of COM-B:
Each element in the COM-B Model plays a crucial role in enabling behavior change:

  • Capability:
    This refers to a person's ability to perform a behavior, including both physical and psychological capabilities. Instructional design strategies often enhance capability by building relevant knowledge and skills through training and practice.

  • Opportunity:
    Opportunity encompasses external factors that make the behavior possible, such as physical environment and social influences. Instructional designers can create opportunities by designing supportive learning environments and encouraging social interactions that facilitate the desired behavior.

  • Motivation:
    Motivation includes both reflective processes (such as goals and intentions) and automatic processes (like impulses and emotions) that drive behavior. Motivation can be strengthened through engaging content, incentives, and clear benefits of the behavior change.
