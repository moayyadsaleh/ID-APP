Five Moments of Need Explained:

  • New Learning: When learners are acquiring new knowledge for the first time, structured training materials (e.g., courses, tutorials, and workshops) are essential. These resources lay the foundation and help establish core competencies.

  • More Learning: As learners build on initial knowledge, they need advanced materials or deep-dive sessions. This phase includes advanced courses, mentoring, and scenario-based learning to reinforce and expand on foundational skills.

  • Applying Knowledge: When learners apply what theyâ€™ve learned in real-world settings, they often require immediate support. Quick reference guides, checklists, and microlearning modules are effective at this stage, providing reminders and refreshing key steps.

  • Dealing with Change: When procedures or tools change, update training resources to bridge knowledge gaps. This can include update modules, virtual demos, or micro-courses that focus specifically on the new processes.

  • Solving Problems: When unexpected issues arise, learners need problem-solving resources. Provide troubleshooting guides, FAQs, discussion forums, and self-help resources that allow learners to resolve issues independently.

Example Course: In a **Customer Service Training Program**, provide an initial onboarding module for new hires, then create advanced modules on conflict resolution and de-escalation. Provide job aids like call scripts for on-the-job support, and create an FAQ repository for troubleshooting. When policies change, update resources to reflect these changes, ensuring relevance.
