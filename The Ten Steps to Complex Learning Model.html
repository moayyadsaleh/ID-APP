The Ten Steps to Complex Learning:
This model outlines ten structured steps that guide the design of instruction for complex learning experiences:

  • 1. Design Learning Tasks:
    Create meaningful, real-life learning tasks that learners will engage with throughout the training.

  • 2. Sequence Task Difficulty:
    Organize tasks from simple to complex, building foundational skills first.

  • 3. Design Supportive Information:
    Provide background information, theories, or conceptual models that support the tasks.

  • 4. Present Procedural Information:
    Offer clear instructions on "how" to perform tasks, especially for procedural skills.

  • 5. Provide Part-Task Practice:
    Create opportunities for focused practice on individual skills.

  • 6. Provide Process-Oriented Feedback:
    Offer feedback focusing on the process, guiding learners to improve their approach.

  • 7. Encourage Reflection:
    Prompt learners to reflect on their experiences to enhance understanding.

  • 8. Design Supportive Scaffolding:
    Implement scaffolding that gradually decreases as learners become more competent.

  • 9. Include Collaborative Learning:
    Encourage collaboration to foster deeper understanding through social learning.

  • 10. Focus on Learner Autonomy:
    Promote independence by encouraging learners to make decisions and self-evaluate.

Course Title: Project Management for New Managers

  • 1. Design Learning Tasks:
    Learners complete a project planning task, simulating real-life projects, such as launching a new product. Tasks include defining project goals, timelines, and resources.

  • 2. Sequence Task Difficulty:
    Start with smaller projects to practice planning and increase complexity with each module, adding elements like risk management and stakeholder communication.

  • 3. Design Supportive Information:
    Provide essential information on project management frameworks (e.g., Agile, Waterfall) and tools like Gantt charts and Kanban boards to guide planning and tracking.

  • 4. Present Procedural Information:
    Offer step-by-step guidance for using project management software, creating timelines, and organizing team tasks, presented as tutorials and job aids.

  • 5. Provide Part-Task Practice:
    Include activities focused on specific project management skills, such as defining tasks, setting deadlines, and managing resources, to build confidence in each area.

  • 6. Provide Process-Oriented Feedback:
    Give detailed feedback on project plans, focusing on planning accuracy, task prioritization, and resource allocation to help learners refine their approaches.

  • 7. Encourage Reflection:
    Incorporate reflection activities after each project, prompting learners to assess their decision-making, challenges faced, and lessons learned.

  • 8. Design Supportive Scaffolding:
    Provide initial checklists and templates for project planning, gradually phasing them out as learners become more comfortable and self-sufficient.

  • 9. Include Collaborative Learning:
    Assign group projects where learners can share ideas, discuss approaches, and support each other in completing a project plan.

  • 10. Focus on Learner Autonomy:
    Allow learners to lead their final project independently, making decisions on timelines, resources, and project scope, with self-assessment tools to gauge their own effectiveness.
