xAPI (Experience API/Tin Can):
â€¢ xAPI, or Experience API, is a tool used to track and record learning experiences both inside and outside traditional learning environments. It uses statements in the format "Actor - Verb - Object" to capture specific learner interactions. Below is a comprehensive list of action verbs commonly used in xAPI statements.

Comprehensive List of xAPI Verbs:

  • Experienced: Used to record when a learner has encountered or completed content.
  • Completed: Indicates that a learner has finished a task, module, or course.
  • Attempted: Logs an attempt by a learner to perform a task or engage with content.
  • Passed: Records that the learner has successfully met the criteria for a task or assessment.
  • Failed: Indicates that the learner did not meet the required performance level.
  • Answered: Used to record the response of a learner to a question or survey.
  • Interacted: Tracks when a learner has engaged with content, such as clicking or dragging items.
  • Watched: Logs that a learner has watched video content or animations.
  • Read: Used to capture when a learner has read an article, document, or other text content.
  • Commented: Indicates that the learner has left a comment, usually in discussion forums or social platforms.
  • Rated: Records that the learner has rated content, often in feedback or review sections.
  • Shared: Logs when a learner has shared content with others, promoting collaborative learning.
  • Scored: Tracks the learnerâ€™s score on a test or activity.
  • Accessed: Used to log when a learner has opened or accessed a resource or module.
  • Searched: Indicates that a learner has searched for content within a system.
  • Connected: Used to capture social interactions, such as following or connecting with peers.
  • Responded: Logs a response, such as answering a prompt or replying in a discussion.
  • Liked: Indicates that a learner has shown a preference for content, often seen in social learning platforms.
  • Viewed: Records when a learner has viewed content, similar to "watched" but for various media.
  • Practiced: Used to track practice activities, such as exercises or skill drills.
  • Contributed: Logs when a learner has added content, such as uploading an assignment or file.
